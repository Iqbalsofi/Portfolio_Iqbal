About Me

Background & Motivation

I completed my B.Tech in Computer Science & Engineering from Amity University, Delhi NCR in 2021. After graduation, I spent three years working in the industry—first as an Associate Software Developer at DXC Technology, and then as a Custom Software Engineering Analyst at Accenture, where I worked extensively with analytics platforms and data pipelines.

While my industry experience gave me strong practical skills in handling large-scale data and building ETL workflows, I found myself increasingly curious about the theoretical foundations of the machine learning models I was using. I wanted to understand not just how to apply these tools, but why they work—from the mathematics of optimization to the architectures of deep neural networks.

This curiosity led me to pursue a Master's in Data Science at Rowan University, where I am now exploring research problems in NLP and Computer Vision. My goal is to continue this journey through a PhD program, where I can contribute to advancing the field while building on my practical experience.

Research Motivation

My research interests emerged from observing gaps between what current ML systems can do and what real-world applications require. During my time at Accenture, I worked with sentiment analysis and user behavior prediction systems. While these systems performed well on standard metrics, I noticed they often struggled with edge cases, domain shifts, and interpretability requirements.

These observations drive my interest in:

  • Transformer architectures and their applications beyond standard benchmarks
  • Model interpretability using techniques like SHAP and attention visualization
  • Cross-domain transfer learning and domain adaptation challenges
  • The intersection of NLP and Computer Vision in multimodal systems
